About the practice

Steam as a space
for deep relaxation
of mind and body

I love seeing how a good steam ceremony affects people. Warmth, silence, and someone nearby who takes care of the process — and the body relaxes on its own. Tension releases, the mind quiets down, and after the session guests emerge in a state where words barely come together. Pure bliss.

It matters to me that the steam room feels safe, cozy, and calm. I pay close attention to that. It works in a simple sauna just as well as in a fully equipped steam room.

Warming up the soul's cold — that's how one of my guests described what happens in the steam room. When there's a frozen response, accumulated tension, coldness in the body — steam helps it come out, thaw.

My Story

The Road to Steam

Born in Vladivostok. I hold a PhD in physics. The research mindset stayed with me here too: I have a solid understanding of what happens to the body in a steam room.

15 years of rhythmic gymnastics. I learned a great deal through the body. Today what matters to me is attention to sensations and respect for boundaries.

My first ceremonial steam happened at a regional Burning Man in 2020. Since then I've trained with various masters, but the greatest teacher has been practice itself.

I've led steam sessions at festivals in Russia, Sweden, the Netherlands, and Portugal. Hundreds of people have come through my steam rooms. Thermal culture across different countries is a whole world, and I find joy in exploring it.

Body therapist and massage practitioner. I work with the body through warmth, touch, and attention. I also practice IFS (Internal Family Systems) — a framework for working with the psyche.

Currently based in Portugal. I hold regular steam ceremonies and travel to festivals and retreats.

How it works

The Steam Ceremony

Group Rounds

Three group rounds in the steam room. We warm up gradually — gently at first, then deeper. Between rounds: rest, tea, silence.

Individual Steaming

After the group part, there are personal slots for those who want to go deeper. Steaming with veniks (leafy oak bundles) with full attention to your body and state.

Integration

Time to be still, to listen to your body. A cold water contrast — for those who feel called to it.

Small groups — up to 5–6 people.
